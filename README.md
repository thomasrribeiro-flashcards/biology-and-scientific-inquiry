# Biology and Scientific Inquiry

A novice-first orientation to biology's unifying ideas and to the evidence
reasoning used to study living systems.

## Scope

- Subject: `biology`
- Learner level: foundational, for a cold-start adult or independent learner
- Confirmed mathematical/tool prerequisites: none
- Confirmed subject prerequisites: none
- Machine-readable deck and tool prerequisites: `deck.toml`
- What mastery enables: identify useful system boundaries and scales; connect
  biology's five crosscutting concepts; distinguish mechanistic explanations
  from need- or intention-based stories; interpret elementary claims,
  observations, comparisons, models, and uncertainty; and enter the later
  quantitative, chemical, cellular, genetic, evolutionary, ecological, and
  research-practice decks without hidden orientation gaps.
- Deliberate exclusions: arithmetic and statistics; chemistry and molecular
  mechanisms; detailed cell biology, genetics, evolution, ecology, anatomy, or
  taxonomy; laboratory and field technique; programming; clinical advice; and
  claims of hands-on research competence.

The deck uses familiar organisms for orientation but does not make humans or a
single model organism the default. A cell, organism, or population introduced
here is an organizational bridge, not a substitute for the later specialist
decks.

## Chapter map

| File | Topic | Learning outcomes |
|---|---|---|
| `01_living_systems_and_scale.md` | Living systems, boundaries, and scale | Choose a question-relevant system boundary and scale; connect explanations across scales; notice variation; prefer mechanisms to need- or intention-based stories; judge elementary evidence and model limits. |
| `02_patterns_of_life.md` | Patterns of life and the limits of definitions | Use a cluster of shared life processes without treating it as an exceptionless checklist; distinguish an organism from a nonliving object and explain why boundary cases require explicit criteria. |
| `03_structure_function_and_constraint.md` | Structure, function, and constraint | Infer how arrangement and material constrain what a biological structure can do; distinguish current function from a claim about origin. |
| `04_information_and_response.md` | Information in living systems | Distinguish stored, transmitted, and detected information; trace an elementary cue-response pathway without assuming genetics or chemistry. |
| `05_matter_energy_and_change.md` | Matter, energy, and living change | Track qualitative inputs, outputs, transfers, and transformations; distinguish matter cycling from energy transfer without calculation. |
| `06_variation_inheritance_and_evolution.md` | Variation, inheritance, and evolution | Explain population change across generations from variation, inheritance, and unequal reproductive outcomes; reject individual striving and goal-directed evolution. |
| `07_interactions_feedback_and_emergence.md` | Interactions, feedback, and emergence | Trace simple interaction networks and feedback; explain why a whole may behave differently from an isolated part. |
| `08_questions_claims_and_predictions.md` | Questions, claims, and testable predictions | Turn observations into bounded questions, distinguish hypotheses from predictions, and state results that would count against an explanation. |
| `09_comparisons_controls_and_causes.md` | Comparisons, controls, and causal inference | Choose informative comparisons and controls, distinguish association from intervention evidence, and bound causal conclusions. |
| `10_evidence_models_and_synthesis.md` | Evidence, models, uncertainty, and synthesis | Weigh converging and conflicting evidence, compare models by purpose and limits, and build a cross-scale explanation without overstating certainty. |

All card prose and technical figures are original. The diagrams represent
relationships rather than biological specimens and are explicitly not to
scale. The pilot makes three bounded simplifications: it treats a system
boundary as a question-dependent analytical choice; it uses `cell`,
`organism`, and `population` only as first-pass scales; and it treats a model
as a purposeful simplification whose omissions must be judged against its use.

Do not commit proprietary reference files. Free access does not by itself grant
permission for AI ingestion, copying, modification, or redistribution.
